Gallery

Surreal Dreamscapes are a mix a photography, graphic design, and original artwork. Each piece is brought to life with it's own unique style and personality. Often working in a collage style experimenting and breaking things, all in the name of creating something new.

The photography is inspired by a cross country trip that spanned roughly 7k miles traveling from Philadephia, south and to the westcoast, before finally resting in the pacific northwest. Each photo is then remixed to bring a brand new perspective to landscape photography.

In addition to photography you will also be able to view and learn more about graphic design pieces inspired by pop culture and original artwork on the gallery page.
“A mind that is stretched by a new experience can never go back to its old dimensions.”
- Oliver Wendell Holmes

About

Born and raised in Reading, Pa, north of Philadelphia, artist, student, photographer, traveler, Matt Lehr welcomes you to Surreal Dreamscapes. Some of his biggest influences include Salvador Dali, Hunter Thompson, and Don Quixote.

Traveling and hitting the open road was always a dream for the artist despite being close with family and friends in PA, adventure was calling. Matt hopes his first cross country trip will not be his last and also has intentions on exploring various parts of the world.

You can learn more about Matt, his adventures and passions on the about page.
