Solo Tiny Home Living

Detached Accessory Dwelling Units (DADU) were originally intended as additional living space family for family members who either could not live in the main house, due to lack of room or else they wanted independence from the rest of hte family. The idea of renting backyard space to a person so they could park and reside in their tiny house on another's property is more a recent idea. Existing property owners are also looing to build ADU's in their backyards, as rentals however, in an attempt to cash in on the vacation rental boom.

Zoning Issuses

If you are planning on building your own tiny home it gets more complicated than just building the home. In Washington State, you can apply for a self-certification license to help make sure your DIY dream meets the state specifications. This does not guarantee it will fit the local zoning requirements though, which are in a state of flux.

The City of Vancouver will be hosting public hearings this year (2017) to consider updating it's existing ADU rules. At this moment in time it is a risk to build and live in a tiny home on your own property or someone else's property until the existing rules are modified.

Tiny Home Communities

For most people, the vision of living in a tiny house on a plot of land in the city is just that, a vision. The rule for manufactured home parks requires the home to be at least 864 square feet in size. That's a home 18' wide and 48 feet long, much larger than the average 10' by 24' THOW (Tiny House on Wheels) that most people are building.

Zoning Issues

Zoning laws usually require the land to have one, large main house and limit the total number of residents to six unrelated people. That does not allow many people to live in a "community". Although change could be on the horizon, and maybe someday soon parks will exist in which tiny home owners will be able to rent a space as a timeshare in a community of tiny houses.

Until that day, the pros and cons of tiny houses will be debated between their advocates and their detractors.
